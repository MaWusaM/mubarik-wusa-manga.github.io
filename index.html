About me

I'm a Junior Data Analyst with a background in Agricultural Biotechnology from KNUST, and practical research experience that spans household surveys, field data collection, and laboratory molecular analysis.

My journey began in the lab — extracting DNA, running PCR procedures for cowpea aphid resistance studies, and working alongside scientists at CSIR-SARI. That foundation gave me a deep respect for rigorous, evidence-based thinking that now drives how I approach data.

During my national service at the Institute for Rural Development and Innovation Studies (IRDIS), I contributed to major projects including an End-of-term evaluation for a UNFPA & UNICEF Adolescent Girls Empowerment Project, the Grain Grading and Certification Project Baseline Survey, and a Strengthening Investments in Gender-Responsive Climate Adaptation (SIGRA) study.

Today, I'm a certified ALX Data Analytics graduate, expanding those skills with Python, SQL, and Power BI to solve real-world problems in agriculture, development, climate, and business.

Projects

Featured Project

Food Security Trends in Ghana (1990–2023)

A Python-based analysis of over 30 years of food security data from the World Bank World Development Indicators. The project examined how population growth, agricultural productivity, fertilizer consumption, and undernourishment interact over time in Ghana.

A custom Food Security Risk Index was developed to assess long-term food security pressure, with full data collection via the World Bank API, cleaning, EDA, correlation analysis, and time-series visualization.

Python Pandas Matplotlib Seaborn Scikit-learn World Bank API
View on GitHub
Key findings
Ghana's population grew steadily over 3 decades — tracked against food supply changes.
Agricultural production increased significantly, correlating with reduced undernourishment.
Fertilizer consumption showed strong positive relationship with food production output.
Food Security Risk Index surfaced periods of high pressure relative to population growth.
